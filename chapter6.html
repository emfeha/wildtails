Chapter 6

British Espionage

Through one of your recent contacts, you arrange a meeting in New L’Oreans with the same British agent you saw with Jean in Cartalena. She promises a handsome payment for a daring mission: acquiring Commodore Gnaw’s battle plans and extensive maps of the area. She provides the location of a secret hideout where you can meet after the raid, offering it to you permanently if you hold up your end of the deal.

The Agent

At the secret hideout on the outskirts of New L’Oreans one of your sailors gets your attention. “The agent, she’s arrived,” he says. “You might want to have a look at this first, though. Just came in.” He hands you a letter stamped with the seal of your wealthy American backer. Glancing over the message, you infer that Pierre Lafayette has been aggressively recruiting mercenaries to take on an unknown threat. Whether this refers to the British or someone else is not entirely clear.

“Welcome to your new accommodations,” the agent announces as she enters. “Britain has no problem with pirates, as long as they don’t interfere too much with our operations. Can we trust you to work alongside us, rather than against us?”

You hand over the stolen documents and maps as you consider your options.

Read both options, then choose one:

Assure the agent that you look forward to a strong alliance with the British.

Make it clear to the agent that Ratuga Bay is not to be trifled with and that your fleet is fully prepared to defend itself when Britain attacks.

Extend Trust

The agent smiles. “I’m so glad to hear that. We’ll be in touch when we have more work for you.”

As she leaves, you consider your decision. Pirate crews are notoriously talkative. Once word gets out that you’ve been working for the British, your contacts will dry up and it’ll be harder to manage your already limited resources.
